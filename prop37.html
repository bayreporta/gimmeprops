Prop 37 would require that any raw or processed food made from plants with genetically modified material to be labeled if sold in California. Such foods could also not be labeled natural in product advertising. There are a number of exceptions to what can be labeled, including products from animals fed GMO material and food sold for immediate consumption, like booze or restaurant grub.

If Prop 37 passes, what will be labeled in stores?

Prop 37 includes several exemptions to the labeling requirement. These exceptions have drawn criticism that the proposed law serves special interests like the meat, dairy and alcohol industries. But advocates argue the labeling process will be easier by including exemptions.
